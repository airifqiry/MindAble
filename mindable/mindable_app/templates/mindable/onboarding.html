Skills & Strengths
What you bring to the table — in your own words.
Step 1
What are you genuinely good at — and what can you do?
Think beyond job titles. Include skills, tools, soft strengths, domains. No formal format needed.
ExampleI'm really good at turning complicated ideas into clear, simple writing. I work well independently and I'm very detail-oriented. Technically: Figma, Adobe Suite, HTML/CSS, basic Python. I pick up new tools fast and I'm great at research.
Work Values
What you're good at isn't the same as what you want.
Step 1.5
What do you want from a job — and what would make you leave?
What makes work feel meaningful? What do you need to stay? What would make you walk out within a few months?
ExampleI want work that actually matters — I can't just grind for a product I don't believe in. Autonomy is huge for me. I'd leave if my accommodations weren't respected, the culture was toxic, or if I had to do back-to-back calls all day.
Neurotype
Your mental health, disability or neurodivergent profile.
Step 2
What mental health condition, disability or neurotype do you have — and how does it show up at work?
Entirely optional, but the more specific you are, the better the AI filters environments that won't work. Never shared with employers.
ExampleI have ADHD and generalised anxiety disorder, diagnosed at 24. I also experience chronic fatigue. In good patches I can hyperfocus for hours. When anxiety is high I struggle to start tasks and need very clear instructions. Loud environments shut me down completely.
Disadvantages
Honesty here filters out jobs that would be wrong for you.
Step 3
What qualities or abilities do you lack — or find genuinely difficult in a work context?
The flip side of your skills section. What tasks, situations or demands are hard or impossible for you right now? This is protective, not judgmental.
ExampleI'm not good at improvising on the spot — I need time to process. I struggle with multitasking and switching between tasks quickly. I can't handle open-plan offices or high-noise environments. Starting tasks without clear instructions is very hard for me.
Success Enablers
Conditions and accommodations that help you do your best work.
Step 4
What environment, accommodations or working conditions do you need to actually thrive?
Communication style, schedule flexibility, environment type, management approach, tools — what would a supportive employer need to know?
ExampleI need all tasks in writing — not verbal. Meetings need 24 hours notice with an agenda. A quiet or private workspace is non-negotiable. Flexible start times (mornings are very hard). Regular written check-ins rather than surprise calls. Async-first communication overall.
